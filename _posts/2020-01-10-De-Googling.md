Around July 2019 I decided to start de-googling myself. For "de-googling" I mean "*the act of reducing my dependence and usage of Google Services*. It can eventually lead to abandoning Google completely, even to the extent of closing the acount forever, or not. It is about diversifying your providers and take back control over the tracking Google does about you.

Before getting deep into details, I wanted to make clear that **I like Google**. A lot. I believe that, as of today (January 7th, 2020), Google is one of those few tech companies which, between Good and Evil, has a net balance that places it in the Good area. Not very far from the neutral point, though, but it's still good. I think Google made Internet a better place, and revolutionised the way we use Web, and many of the services that run over Internet (beginning --but not only-- with the e-mail), the same as Apple revolutionised most of the physical devices and concepts it touched. That was 100% clear in the early 2000s, when the "don't be evil" precept was clearly true and working, and even now that Google is know for having turned into quite evil and dodgy activities related to user tracking, and with regards to its employees,... I still believe that Google is good (lowercase g) and should be able to return to its root values of the early 2000s.

But one thing is to believe that Google is (still) good, and a very different thing is to be tightly coupled to its services. Therefore, I think that it's a very good idea to decouple yourself from Google and limit its usage to when it's absolutely necessary, as you would do with any other service provider.

And it's never too late. My Google Account was opened in 2004, with the first beta accounts available for GMail, and it was my main service provider for 15 years of *quite intensive* Internet usage. My dependence of Google, my "Google lock-in" was phenomenal.

This is a very long post.
